SCRUM Information

What is Scrum?

Scrum is a framework that helps teams work together! Scrum is like a rugby team, this is where the name scrum comes from. Scrum encourages teams to learn through experiences, self-organize while working on problems and reflecting on the wins and losses.

While Scrum is used frequently by a lot of software development teams, its principles and lessons can be applied to all kinds of teamwork. This is one of the reasons Scrum is so popular around the industry. Scrum describes a set of meetings, tools and roles that work in conjunction with each other, allowing teams to structure and manage the work.

How we used Scrum

The team has used Scrum methodology to create the QA Cinema Application. The project started with reading the specification and breaking it down into tasks, which were then placed in the backlog on our Jira Board. After all of the items were in the backlog we used Plan-It Poker to plan out our sprint and assign tasks to each team member.

Following the Scrum Methodology and Agile, we had daily stand-ups, where we would speak about what we have done, what we were going to do and if we had any blockers, this allowed us to identify blockers earlier on, and ensured a cohesive teamwork throughout the project.

At the end of each Sprint, we had a review and retrospect, to look at things we have done, how well we have completed each item, and what we could have improved on.
